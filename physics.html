1. Laws of Motion

The laws of motion, formulated by Sir Isaac Newton, are fundamental to understanding how forces affect the movement of objects. These laws help describe the relationship between the object and the forces acting on it.

2. Work, Energy, and Power

Work, energy, and power are closely related concepts in physics that describe how forces cause objects to move. Work is done when a force moves an object over a distance, energy is the ability to do work, and power is the rate at which work is done.

3. Gravitation

Gravitation is the force of attraction between all masses in the universe. Sir Isaac Newton's law of universal gravitation describes this force, and it is responsible for phenomena like the falling of objects to the ground and the orbits of planets.
