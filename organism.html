üìå Introduction

Biodiversity refers to the variety of life on Earth, including plants, animals, and microorganisms. It helps maintain ecological balance and provides essential resources for human survival.

üåø Classification of Living Organisms

üìå Five Kingdom Classification (By R.H. Whittaker)

  • Monera: Unicellular, Prokaryotic (e.g., Bacteria)
  • Protista: Single-celled Eukaryotic (e.g., Amoeba)
  • Fungi: Non-photosynthetic (e.g., Yeast, Mushroom)
  • Plantae: Autotrophic (e.g., Algae, Flowering Plants)
  • Animalia: Heterotrophic (e.g., Humans, Birds)

üî¨ Types of Biodiversity

  • Genetic Diversity: Variation within species (e.g., different breeds of dogs).
  • Species Diversity: Different species in an ecosystem (e.g., various bird species).
  • Ecosystem Diversity: Variety of ecosystems (e.g., forests, deserts, oceans).
